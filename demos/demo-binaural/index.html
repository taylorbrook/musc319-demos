Spatial II: hear one sound move around your head

One source is rendered to two channels for headphones through a head-related transfer function (HRTF), the binaural model that places a sound in 3D. Move it with the sliders or by dragging on the top-down view, or press Orbit. Then flip the rendering from HRTF to plain stereo pan and hear the scene collapse to a flat line inside your head. Use headphones: binaural rendering only works over headphones. Press Start, then Play.

Step 1: enable sound

Audio is off.

Step 2: rendering mode (the A/B that matters)
HRTF: full 3D, including height and front/back.
Step 3: place and move the source

In HRTF mode the source sounds outside your head, and you can hear it pass behind you and rise overhead, because the model bakes in the time and level difference between your ears plus the spectral colouring of your outer ear. In stereo pan mode the same path becomes a flat left-right fade with no height and no behind, glued between the headphone drivers. A binaural render is how the 8-channel concert work reaches a listener who only has headphones, which is the immersive deliverable for A4.
