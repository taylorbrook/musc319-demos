No gesture recorded yet. Record one, then compare the two replays.

The performed replay rides your continuous motion, so the filter glides and pauses the way your hand did. The stepped replay holds each value to the nearest grid position, the way step automation or a quantized lane would, so it jumps instead of glides. A coarse grid (fewer steps) makes the contrast obvious; a fine grid approaches the performed line. That is the choice live control gives a composer: let the gesture breathe, or lock it to the grid, the same material either way.
