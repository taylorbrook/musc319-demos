FM synthesis: two oscillators, many partials

One carrier oscillator's frequency is modulated by a second, the modulator. That modulation adds sidebands around the carrier. The modulation index controls how bright (how many sidebands); the carrier:modulator ratio controls whether the result is pitched (harmonic) or bell-like (inharmonic). Press Start, then Play, and move the two sliders.

The spectrum (live FFT)

Raise the index: sidebands spread out from the carrier (brighter). Set an integer ratio (1, 2, 3…) and the sidebands land on harmonics (pitched); set a non-integer ratio and they fall between, giving the metallic, bell-like spectra FM is known for.
