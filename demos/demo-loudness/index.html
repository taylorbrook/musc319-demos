Mastering: why louder does not win

A short looping beat runs through a master chain: Drive pushes it into a brickwall limiter. The meters show the produced loudness, the peak, and the dynamics left between them. Turn on Platform normalization and push the Drive: the produced loudness climbs and the dynamics shrink, but the playback loudness holds at the target. Press Start, then Play.

The waveform is the mastered signal. As you raise the Drive it fills toward the ceiling and the transients flatten: that is the dynamic range you are spending. The loudness meter is an approximation of LUFS, not a certified meter, and the peak is a sample peak, not a true inter-sample peak.
