AM & ring modulation: sidebands and inharmonic spectra

A carrier tone is multiplied by a modulator. In amplitude modulation the carrier survives and a sideband pair appears around it; in ring modulation the carrier is removed, leaving the metallic, inharmonic pair that classic electronic timbres use. Watch the spectrum as you switch modes and move the modulator frequency. Press Start, then Play.

In AM you see three peaks: the carrier plus a sideband above and below it. Switch to RM and the centre carrier disappears, leaving the two sidebands — that removal is what makes ring modulation sound metallic and inharmonic.
