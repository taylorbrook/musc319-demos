Spatialization: hear a sound move around your head

One sound source is fed through the Web Audio panner. Move it by azimuth (around you), elevation (up and down), and distance, then switch the panning model. HRTF (binaural) places the source in real 3D using the same cues a head gives (time, level, and ear-colour), so on headphones it sounds outside your head. Stereo is a plain left/right level pan that stays inside your head. Use headphones for the HRTF mode. Press Start, then Play, then Orbit.

Step 1: enable sound (headphones recommended)

Audio is off.

Step 2: choose the panning model (switch while it plays)

Step 3: place the source

The source is a short noise burst. Broadband transients carry the timing and head-shadow cues far better than a steady tone, which is why a click train localizes more sharply than a hum.

Try this: set the model to Stereo and orbit. The sound only slides left to right, and stays in your head. Switch to HRTF and orbit again: on headphones the source now travels around and behind you, and you can raise it overhead with elevation. That difference is what a spatial render adds over a pan.
